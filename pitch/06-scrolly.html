It starts as one line

Anyone files a story: a bug, a request, half a thought. It costs nothing — the AI can't even see it yet.

A human types /admit

One comment from the PO or team lead pulls it into Research. The grill skills read the docs and the code, then interrogate the story until it can't be misread.

The AI hands back — it never guesses

A finished plan waits under pending-approval. An unsettled design question waits as one specific question under needs-clarification. Wrong plan? /revise sends it back with your notes attached.

/approve/revise/redo

Ready becomes a fact

/approve means the Definition of Ready is met — testable, TDD-ordered acceptance criteria, a routed repo, linked dependencies, an assigned sprint. Identical on every team. A script, not a person, keeps the queue honest.

Built while you sleep

Each night, per repo, the TDD agent takes the top of the queue: failing test first, minimum code, refactor. A story that won't go green is dropped cleanly and re-queued — the branch is never red in the morning.

A human lands every line

Morning brings one reviewable PR per repo, opening with a mandatory Deviations & Decisions section. An engineer reviews against the approved criteria and merges by hand. Agents have no merge rights — by permission, not by promise.

Accepted, with receipts

The PO closes it at sprint review. The story carries its whole history: the analysis, the approval, the criteria, the PR, the changelog entry. The work item is the audit trail.

Act II — Who does what.Three layers, structurally separated.

Humans keep every judgment call

Admit, approve, revise, merge, accept — plus every design decision and every priority. Each gate names its role: PO for features, team lead for stories.

Agents do the labor between gates

Grilling, planning, test-driven implementation, PR authoring, CI babysitting. No design authority, no merge rights, no exceptions.

Scripts hold the guardrails

Command parsing and role checks, queue selection, dashboards — plain, unit-tested code. The rules that keep the pipeline safe are never left to a model's judgment.

Overnight

The gatekeeper sweeps commands, the queue selects, the agent builds. By morning there's a green PR per repo — capacity that used to be idle.

Morning

Newly admitted work gets researched, and the digest lands before standup: queue health, and a "your move" list per role.

Evening

Type /approve from anywhere — a phone in the elevator counts. The sweep applies it, and tonight's build picks it up.

Act IV — It federates.Many small pipelines, one shared design.

Portfolio: features gate on quarters

grill-azdo-feature forces scope, owning team, and story split before a feature can commit to a quarter. The PO approves; child stories land in the team's backlog.

Teams own their own gates

Each team runs its own gatekeeper, allowlist, and caps. Planning authority is handed down with the stories — no central bottleneck.

Repos opt in, one by one

A nightly builder per repository, one PR per repo per night. CI, branch protection, and release tooling stay exactly as each repo runs them today.

Cross-team work coordinates itself

Dependencies are predecessor links, created during Research. A story is never eligible until its predecessors are Done — enforced by the queue script, every night, across teams.
