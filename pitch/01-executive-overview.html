Agentic Delivery Pipeline · Document 1 of 3 · Executive Overview

Humans decide. AI executes. Scripts enforce.

A production-proven operating model where every piece of work flows through explicit human decision gates, and everything between those gates — analysis, planning, test-driven implementation, reporting — is done autonomously by AI agents on a fixed daily schedule. The human role compresses to minutes per day of high-leverage judgment; delivery continues overnight.

The operating loop at a glance

Work items move through a small, visible state machine. Nothing passes a decision gate except an authorized human's short comment command; between gates, AI advances the work on a fixed schedule with no human in the loop. This is currently running end-to-end in a real shipping project (an Android game), with one owner steering it from a phone.

The three-layer trust model

The design principle that makes this safe to run unattended: each layer does only what it is best at, and the boundaries between layers are structural, not behavioral promises.

  • Control without ceremony. Every decision an executive would want a human to make still has a human making it — but the decision costs one comment, not a meeting.
  • Predictable spend. AI runs on a fixed schedule with a hard nightly cap; expensive model time is used only where judgment is needed, while parsing, queueing, and reporting are near-free scripts.
  • Audit-grade traceability. Every item carries its analysis, its approval, its acceptance criteria, its PR, and its changelog entry — the work item is the paper trail.
  • Quality is enforced upstream. Work isn't eligible to build until it has human-approved, testable acceptance criteria; the builder must develop test-first and keeps CI green while the PR awaits review.
  • Fails safe. Unauthorized commands are ignored, re-runs are idempotent, a failed build drops out cleanly and re-queues, and the AI can never merge its own work.
