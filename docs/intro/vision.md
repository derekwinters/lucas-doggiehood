# Vision

## The pitch

Doggiehood is a neighborhood of dogs, viewed from above. You don't control a character — you're an unseen presence panning and zooming around the streets, tapping on dogs who have something to say. Some dogs wander the sidewalks. Some watch from a window. Each one has a name, a breed, and a personality that colors how it talks and what it asks for.

Helping a dog — finding what it lost, buying what it wants, clearing out its bug problem — earns a little currency, which goes right back into the neighborhood: gifts for dogs, comfort decorations for their yards, and (in v0.4) whole new streets.

## Tone

- **Cozy, not competitive.** No fail states, no timers, no punishment for ignoring a quest. ([#28](https://github.com/derekwinters/lucas-doggiehood/issues/28))
- **All-ages.** Built for a family to enjoy together, not designed around any one age bracket specifically. ([#43](https://github.com/derekwinters/lucas-doggiehood/issues/43))
- **A real place, not a store.** Free, offline, no ads, no accounts. ([#41](https://github.com/derekwinters/lucas-doggiehood/issues/41), [#42](https://github.com/derekwinters/lucas-doggiehood/issues/42))
- **Bright and a little goofy.** Bold saturated color, chunky sticker-book UI, a whimsical soundtrack — see [Art & UI Style](../specs/world/art-style.md).

## Where the name comes from

"Doggiehood" — a neighborhood of dogs. The starting cast (Zeus, Nala, Bailey, Sunny, Pepper, Duke, Scout, Waffles) and a set of easter-egg dogs named after real dogs Lucas knows are detailed in [Dog Roster & Names](../specs/dogs/roster-names.md).

## Who's building it, and how

Derek handles structural/technical decisions; Lucas drives creative direction on the dogs, quests, and world. Design conversations happen live and get distilled into GitHub issues immediately, organized under epics and numbered milestones. See [Project Conventions](conventions.md) for how that organization works, and [Engineering](../engineering/tech-stack.md) for how the actual Unity project gets built — by a dedicated development agent working test-first against those issues, not hand-written by either of them.
